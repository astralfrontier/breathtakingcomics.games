> "The courtroom doesn't have enemies. Just the law, the truth, and getting the two to meet."

Shamia "Sam" Echevarra was born with a unique gift: hyper-intelligence.
Rather than becoming an inventor, scientist, or super-detectives,
she found that her aptitude was in law, language, and rhetoric.

Today she's a full-time attorney.
She often works pro bono on behalf of vigilantes or needy members of downtrodden communities.
Her legal genius has led to brilliant courtroom strategies,
ironclad contractual agreements, even model legislation
that have promoted the heroes she supports,
and done justice to the villains she's encountered.

Her usual opponents are wealthy oppressors, soulless corporations,
shadowy government agencies, even powerful villains.
She's helped thousands of families rebuild after a villain attack,
when the insurance company dragged its heels.
She's even gone up against demons and the Fair Folk,
deciphering the intricate terms of such cunning supernatural entities.
Hermetic mages have occasionally wondered if she'd be as good a spellcaster
as she is a lawyer, but so far nobody's put that question to the test.

Thanks to grateful benefactors and being a top-drawer lawyer in a profitable profession,
Sam is independently wealthy.
She needs it.
She's a constant target due her activism,
and her pro bono work still comes with considerable expenses.
Still, heroism has never been about making money,
and Sam is committed to wielding the law the way other heroes wield their abilities.

# Using {frontmatter.name}

Sam can keep heroes out of trouble, or advise them on a good course of legal action.
Beyond that, she can give heroes a mission to gather evidence for one of her cases.

She might work with detectives like <World id="support/moose-orourke" />
and <World id="heroes/the-fractal" />, or other lawyers such as district attorney Amy Chen (one half of <World id="heroes/tao" />).

Sam is inspired by characters like **Jessica Jones** or **Daredevil** aka Matt Murdock,
except operating full-time in the court system rather than acting as a vigilante.

## Plots

- Help Sam in a court case, by gathering evidence or safeguarding a witness
- Protect Sam from an old enemy with a grudge, perhaps with hitmen or henchmen
- Obey Sam's restrictions while dealing with a villain, so she can finally secure a conviction

# Variations

## Other Powers

As written, Sam only has a focused super-intelligence.

- What if she also has other powers - enough that she could function as a superhero herself?
  She might become a vigilante, but she could just as easily feel the risk to her legal career is too great,
  and act scrupulously within the laws governing heroes.
- What if the hints about magic are true, and Sam's genius has let her master spellcasting or summoning
  the way she knows mundane law?
  People coming after her might be in for a rude surprise if she has demons or curses at the ready -
  but would this negatively impact her otherwise-spotless public image?
